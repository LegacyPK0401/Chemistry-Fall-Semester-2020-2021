  • Week 4: Intro to Chemistry Continued

    • On the fourth week of chemistry, we further enhanced how knowledge of chemistry and the implications chemistry has on the real world. One thing we learned about was calorimetry. Calorimetry "is the process of measuring the amount of heat released or absorbed during a chemical reaction." Basicially, calorimetry is when a scientist measures the amount of heat a substance can absorb or distribute. Measuring the heat of a subtance allows the scientist to determine the energy content in that substance. Calorimetry is primarily used in food science to see the energy content of a food item.

    • On the fifth week of chemistry, we learned about the uses of math in science and the transference of energy. We learnedthat "math + science = chemistry." We also learned about the units that are used in chemistry and the International System of Units. Which was created in the 1960s based on the metric system. We also discussed the importance of having a standard set of units for everyone so that no one gets confused. On the second class of the week, we learned about exothermic and endothermic energy and differences between them. As a brief summary, exothermic is when energy is released and the temperature in the surroundings rise. While endothermic is when energy is consumed and the temperature in the surroundings lower.
